# Abstract

We investigate the representation stability of replay metrics in adaptive systems. Using four classes of adaptive systems — distributed coordination networks, immune signaling networks, ant colony stigmergy, and institutional trust networks — we measure how organizational replay stability (G) and historical residue coupling (H) survive representational transformations.

**Key findings:**
1. All embeddings are effectively one-dimensional (participation ratio ≈ 1.0), despite nominal dimensionalities of 6-13.
2. G is fragile: survival rate 47% across perturbation types.
3. H is degenerate: saturated at ≈1.0 for 3/4 systems, making it non-identifiable.
4. The previously reported G∝1/H correlation (r = −0.951) is an artifact of embedding degeneracy and H saturation, not a structural feature.

We introduce a Metric Survival Taxonomy classifying observables as Stable, Conditional, Fragile, or Degenerate. This taxonomy provides a framework for assessing metric identifiability in adaptive systems. The central scientific object is no longer the correlation itself, but the conditions under which replay metrics remain identifiable across representational transformations.
