# Abstract

We present a systematic investigation of organizational persistence across four classes of adaptive systems: distributed coordination networks, immune signaling networks, ant colony stigmergy, and institutional trust networks. We discover a robust empirical law relating organizational replay stability (G) to historical residue coupling (H):

$$G \propto \frac{1}{H}$$

with cross-domain correlation r = −0.951. This law holds across systems with fundamentally different coordination mechanisms, suggesting a universal constraint on persistent adaptive organization. We identify a representation ceiling beyond which scalar observables collapse, motivating the geometric formalism developed in the companion paper.
