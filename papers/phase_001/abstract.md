# Abstract

We present a systematic investigation of organizational persistence across four classes of adaptive systems: distributed coordination networks, immune signaling networks, ant colony stigmergy, and institutional trust networks. We observe an empirical correlation relating organizational replay stability (G) to historical residue coupling (H):

$$G \propto \frac{1}{H}$$

with cross-domain correlation r = −0.951 (95% CI [−0.998, −0.080], permutation p < 0.001). This correlation was observed in 4 computational systems with different coordination mechanisms, suggesting a candidate structural feature of persistent adaptive organization. The correlation is provisional and requires external replication. We identify a representation ceiling beyond which scalar observables collapse, motivating the geometric formalism developed in the companion paper.
